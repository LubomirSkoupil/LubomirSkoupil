Tools

  • Stable and precise PnL is at the heart of front office and middle office processes and controls. During my time at Ezpada I had the opportunity to work on several concepts of various types of PnL including energy products (standard profiles, shapes, transmission rights, multicurrency portfolios), FX and standardized exchange-traded commodity contracs.

    In 2015 I designed significant part of the PnL system Ezpada uses to this day - the system was built on reliable forward curves (one curve per grid, hourly granularity for power and gas), PnL calculated on position rather than individual tickets and on Total-to-Day PnL differences.

    In paralell to the official PnL implemented by IT I created several SQL and Python tools helping us explain and troubleshoot the official PnL (for example tool showing traders positions carried from one day to another and detail of PnL the positions generated between these days).

  • Value at Risk (VaR)
  • Stress testing

  • Reliable forward curves (FWC) with hourly granularity are a must-have for a well performing PnL and other Risk processes (credit, volatility and correlation estimation and modelling, cash flow projections).

    I cooperated with IT on implementation of FWC for power and gas grids and I introduced several stabilizing concepts into the algorithm:

    • price source prioritization (for example "CZ: Trayport > PXE > brokers"; this way if Trayport marking fails and PXE does not publish prices, broker marks would be used for calculation and only small adjustment, if any, will be needed)
    • discarding the least liquid product prices in case of conflict (in March there will sometimes be quotes for Q2, Apr, May and June contracts, where the weighted average of monthly quotes does not equal quote for Q2, which creates confusion; this gest solved by disregarding the least liquid product quote - in this instance June)
    • including seasonality: this increases precision of the curves as it decreases valuation jumps for products that are becomming more liquid

  • Cash Flow projections
  • EoD automatic marking
